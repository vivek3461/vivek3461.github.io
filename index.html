About Me

I am currently a Research Associate in the Guidance, Navigation, and Control (GNC) Laboratory at the Indian Institute of Technology Madras. My current work focuses on the coordinated net capture of an adversarial aerial target, where I contribute to the development of real-time guidance and control strategies for multi-agent autonomous systems.

I completed my Ph.D. in Aerospace Engineering from IIT Madras. My doctoral research, titled "Proportional Navigation-based Guidance Strategies Towards Containment of a Non-maneuvering Target in Planar Engagements", investigated cooperative guidance mechanisms for containing targets using analytical models and simulation-driven validation.

My research interests span guidance and control theory, multi-agent coordination, and motion planning algorithms. I am also deeply interested in sensors, modeling of dynamical systems, and optimal control. As I explore motion planning for autonomous vehicles, I find great motivation in its overlaps with robotics, especially in areas like robotic manipulation, drone navigation, and multi-robot coordination.
