About Me

I am currently a Research Associate in the Guidance, Navigation, and Control (GNC) Laboratory at the Indian Institute of Technology Madras. My current work focuses on the coordinated net capture of an adversarial aerial target, where I contribute to the development of coordinated guidance and control strategies for multiple UAVs to carry a net and approach the target.

I completed my Ph.D. in the Department of Aerospace Engineering at IIT Madras. My doctoral research, titled "Proportional Navigation-based Guidance Strategies Towards Containment of a Non-maneuvering Target in Planar Engagements", involved developing time and angle-constrained guidance strategies for containing targets, leveraging the Proportional Navigation (PN) guidance law.

My research interests span guidance and control of autonomous vehicles, multi-agent coordination, and motion planning algorithms. As I explore motion planning for autonomous UAVs, I find great motivation in its overlaps with robotic applications, especially in areas like robotic manipulation.

I am also deeply interested in optimal control of robotic systems and integration of sensors' information that goes in to the guidance block. Beyond engineering, I find joy in exploring mathematics for its own elegance and challenge, often driven by curiosity more than necessity.
